The beginning

A gap in the market.
A team across the world.

The idea for Steller was simple. Businesses that wanted to offer digital gift cards — loyalty programs, employee rewards, consumer promotions — had no clean way to connect to the vendors who supplied them. There was a gap in the market for a reliable B2B middleware: a layer that sat between integration partners and gift card vendors, handled the wallets, processed the orders, and returned the PINs.

I saw the gap. I knew how to close deals, structure pricing, and bring partners on board. What I could not do was write software. So I did what most non-technical founders do: I outsourced. A development team from the Middle East, working on a project basis. The total engagement: $10,000.

The team was talented. They designed a technically ambitious architecture — a modern microservices stack built on ASP.NET Core 9, PostgreSQL, RabbitMQ for asynchronous processing, Vue.js dashboards. By April 2025, the foundational database was live: 36 tables, the full data model for an order management system. By July, security features were implemented. By November, soft delete for compliance. By December, the frontend dashboards were taking shape.

On paper, it looked like a real product was being built.

Pre-April 2025
Architecture defined
Microservices stack locked: ASP.NET Core 9, PostgreSQL, RabbitMQ, Vue.js. Bamboo Card Portal API as the vendor integration target.
April 2, 2025
Core data model live
Initial migration deployed. 36 tables: users, orders, inventory, wallets, brands, catalog. The foundation of a real system.
May–November 2025
Feature build: currency, security, compliance
Multi-currency rate tables. Password reset flows. Soft-delete audit trail. The system was growing. The team was building.
Late 2025
Frontend dashboards begun
Vue.js admin and consumer dashboards enter development, suggesting backend was approaching feature-completeness.
December 6, 2025
Final sync migration
A maintenance migration to synchronize all environments. Typically a signal that deployment is approaching.
